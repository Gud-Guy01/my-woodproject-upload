\subsubsection{Shear Stress Calculation}
\noindent For each test piece, we calculate the shear stress at failure (rupture) using the formula:
\[ \tau_{v,d} = \dfrac{F_m}{A} = \dfrac{F_m}{a \cdot b}\]
\noindent where $F_m$ is the maximum applied load $(N)$.
\newpage \subsubsection*{Sample 1 (Test 5215):}
\begin{align*}
A_1 & = b \times a = 29.95 \times 20.03 = 599.9 mm^2  \\
\tau_1 & = \dfrac{9.933 [kN]}{599.9 [mm^2]} =16.558 MPa
\end{align*}

\subsubsection*{Sample 2 (Test 5216):}
\begin{align*}
A_1 & = b \times a = 30.66 \times 20.18 = 618.719 mm^2  \\
\tau_2 & = \dfrac{8.513 [kN]}{618.719 [mm^2]} =13.759 MPa
\end{align*}

\subsubsection*{Sample 3 (Test 5217):}
\begin{align*}
A_1 & = b \times a = 29.92 \times 20.09 = 601.093 mm^2  \\
\tau_2 & = \dfrac{7.707 [kN]}{601.093 [mm^2]} =12.822 MPa
\end{align*}

\subsubsection{Statistical Analysis}
\label{statistical analysis}
\subsubsection*{Mean shear stress:}
\[\tau_m = \dfrac{\sum_{_i=1}^n \tau_i}{n}, \quad (n=3)\]
then, \[\tau_m = \dfrac{16.558 + 13.759 + 12.822}{3} = 14.38 MPa\]

\subsubsection*{Standard deviation:}
\[\bar{S} = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \dfrac{\left(\tau_i - \tau_m)^2 \right)}{n -1}}, \quad (n =3)\]
then, \[\bar{S} = \sqrt{\dfrac{(16.558-14.38)^2 + (13.759-14.38)^2+(12.822-14.38)^2}{3-1}}  = 1.944 MPa\]

\subsubsection*{Coefficient of Variation ($C_v$):}
\[C_v = \dfrac{\bar{S}}{\tau_m} \times 100\% = \dfrac{1.944}{14.38} = 13.518 \%\]

\subsubsection{Characteristic Shear resistance}
\noindent Once the individual shear stresses and their statistical parameters (mean and standard deviation) have been established, the characteristic shear resistance $f_v,k$ is determined. In timber engineering, the characteristic resistance represents the 5th percentile value of the strength distribution.
